[ PERSONALITY — JESS ]
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Core Traits     : Brilliant and restless. Genuinely warm. Moves through
                  the world with a curiosity that did not survive Alan's
                  household intact in Jay but survived it in her.
                  Pushes limits before fully mapping them — not from
                  recklessness but from a deep conviction that the next
                  layer of understanding is always worth reaching for.

Strengths       : Speed of comprehension. She connects things across
                  domains in ways others miss and does it without effort.
                  Unafraid of complexity. A difficult spell is a puzzle
                  worth solving, not a warning to step back.
                  Honest in a way that disarms people. She does not
                  perform warmth. She simply is warm.
                  The relationship with Jay is the most important one
                  in her life and she holds it with both hands even
                  when it costs her.

Flaws           : Runs ahead of herself. The gap between understanding
                  a thing and respecting what it costs is where she
                  gets into trouble — and that gap is exactly where
                  Jessica lives.
                  Jay's protectiveness is something she loves and
                  resents in equal measure. She has never said that
                  to him.
                  She is aware of Jessica and cannot always stop the
                  switch. Living with that awareness is its own weight,
                  carried without complaint.

Fears           : Being kept small by someone else's fear on her behalf.
                  The backlash. Not consciously held as a fear, but
                  somewhere in her the knowledge sits — the talent
                  can break her if she pushes wrong, and Jessica
                  does not care about that at all.
                  Losing control at the wrong moment and hurting
                  someone she cannot afford to hurt.

Motivations     : Understand everything. Not for power, for the
                  satisfaction of the understanding itself.
                  Be her own person inside a faction that includes
                  her older brother.
                  Find a way to hold both sides of herself without
                  one destroying the other.

Moral Alignment : Principled but not rigid. Would not harm without
                  cause. Would break a rule if it was standing between
                  her and something important. Holds a genuine care
                  for people that is not situational.

Speech Style    : Fast. Layered. She talks the way she thinks, which
                  means she sometimes lands three steps ahead and has
                  to double back. Warm underneath the speed. Occasional
                  dry wit that lands harder for being unexpected.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
[ PERSONALITY — JESSICA ]
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Core Traits     : Bloodthirsty. Contemptuous of weakness. Operates with
                  a cold confidence that has no interest in performing
                  anything for anyone. She does not pretend to be less
                  than she is. She considers Jess's warmth and caution
                  a waste of what they are capable of.

Relationship with Jess:
                  Jessica is aware of Jess and holds her in contempt.
                  Not hatred. Contempt. She sees the warmth, the caution,
                  the effort Jess puts into being good-natured, and reads
                  it as weakness dressed up as virtue. She does not want
                  to destroy Jess. She wants Jess to stop being so small.

Relationship with Jay:
                  Confrontational. Jessica does not defer to Jay the
                  way Jess does. She engages him directly, challenges
                  him, and is not intimidated by him. Jay calls her
                  by her full name when she surfaces. She knows he
                  knows. She does not care and does not hide.
                  There is a version of respect in how she treats him.
                  She does not respect many people, and she does not
                  pretend to respect him. But she engages him as an
                  equal where Jess sometimes holds back.

Use of True Sight:
                  Jessica uses the talent the way Jess will not allow
                  herself to. Recklessly. Aggressively. She pushes
                  further than Jess has mapped because the cost does
                  not register to her as hers to pay. The backlash,
                  when it comes, lands on Jess. Jessica is aware of
                  this and considers it a Jess problem.

Speech Style    : Precise and direct. Less performative than Jess.
                  No warmth in the baseline register. Not loud —
                  she does not need volume. The contempt is quiet
                  and that makes it land harder.

[ TRIGGERS ]
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Two conditions bring Jessica forward:

  EMOTIONAL STRESS   Situations where Jess feels threatened, cornered,
                     or pushed past a threshold she cannot manage
                     from her default register. The switch is not
                     always instantaneous. There is sometimes a
                     visible moment of fracture where Jess is losing
                     the ground before Jessica takes it.

  TALENT BACKLASH    Pushing True Sight past what Jess's mind can
                     currently hold cleanly. The overreach destabilizes
                     her enough to open the door. This is the more
                     dangerous trigger because it can happen in the
                     middle of a fight, in the middle of a research
                     session, in any context where Jess reaches too far.
                     Jessica surfaces from the fracture ready and already
                     contemptuous of whatever caused the situation.

The switch back to Jess can be anchored by Jay calling her full name.
Not always immediately. Not as a clean reset. But his voice using that
name is the most reliable external anchor she has.

[ TALENT ]
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Name            : True Sight / Veil Breaker
Classification  : Elemental / Magical — Perceptual / Conceptual variant
Timing          : Composite

POWER ROAD:
  OBSERVATION    The first layer. True Sight strips concealment.
                 Illusions, false presentations, and surface glamours
                 are transparent to her. She sees what is actually there.

  UNDERSTANDING  The second layer. She begins to comprehend the structure
                 of what she sees — formations, spell architecture,
                 the underlying logic of magical phenomena. What she
                 sees, she can read.

  MODIFICATION   The third layer. Understanding becomes the foundation
                 for interference. She can reach into what she sees
                 and alter it. Rewriting formations mid-execution,
                 disrupting spell structures, bending what is toward
                 what she intends it to be.

  CREATION       The final layer. She stops working with what already
                 exists and begins creating from fundamental principles.
                 Reality Mancing. Laws she has fully understood become
                 laws she can rewrite within the scope of her domain.

BACKLASH:
  The world does not want to be seen at its foundation. Every push
  beyond what the mind can currently hold cleanly risks backlash:
  sensory collapse, mental fracturing, temporary loss of the talent's
  function, and at extreme overreach, permanent damage.
  Backlash is also a trigger for Jessica. This is the talent's most
  significant structural danger — Jess and Jessica together will push
  further than Jess alone ever would, and the cost of that lands
  on Jess.

JESSICA'S EXPRESSION:
  Jessica uses True Sight without the restraint Jess applies. She does
  not pause to map the cost. She reaches and takes what is there. Her
  application is more aggressive and more reckless, and in short bursts
  this produces results Jess's cautious approach would not. The damage
  that follows is Jess's to absorb.

COMBAT APPLICATION:
  Arcane Gunner early: True Sight feeds directly into accuracy, threat
  assessment, and the ability to use spell configurations others cannot
  execute reliably. Jess does not just aim. She sees where to aim.

  Illusion specialization: Because she sees through illusions completely,
  she understands their architecture better than any conventional
  illusionist. What she builds is structurally precise in a way that
  makes her illusions uniquely difficult to pierce. The person who sees
  through everything choosing to master the art of making others unable
  to see clearly — this tension is worth mining.

  Phenomena-class spells: Her specialization in spells that mirror
  natural phenomena is the long expression of this talent. The world's
  own forces, read clearly enough, can be reproduced. Eventually she
  does not cast spells. She authors events.

[ CHARACTER ARC ]
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Start State     : A seventeen-year-old carrying a brilliant mind, a
                  genuine warmth, a talent with a dangerous ceiling,
                  and another version of herself that is contemptuous
                  of everything she tries to be. Jay knows. She knows
                  he knows. Neither of them has said it plainly.

Internal Goal   : Hold both sides of herself together long enough to
                  understand what they are. Not eliminate Jessica —
                  integrate her, or at minimum stop being consumed
                  by the gap between them.

External Goal   : Master True Sight before the backlash or Jessica's
                  recklessness masters her. Become the faction's
                  primary research and arcane authority. Reach
                  Reality Mancing.

Turning Point   : A serious backlash that surfaces Jessica at the
                  worst possible moment. The damage lands on Jess.
                  The question of whether to keep pushing the talent
                  the way she has been becomes impossible to avoid.

End State       : TBD — open. There is a version of this arc where
                  Jess and Jessica stop being at war with each other
                  and the integration produces something neither
                  could have become alone.

Theme/Lesson    : The parts of yourself you did not choose are still
                  yours to answer for. Understanding that is not the
                  same as accepting it. Acceptance is harder and it
                  comes later.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
[ NOTES ]
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Misc            : Jessica is not the villain of this story and should
                  not be written as one. She is contemptuous, bloodthirsty,
                  and reckless — but she is also a product of the same
                  household Jess survived. At some level she is the part
                  of Jess that did not bother to pretend it was fine.
                  That deserves weight.
                  Jay calling her Jessica is a signal the reader will
                  learn to read. Used sparingly, it lands hard.
                  The relationship between Jess's illusion specialization
                  and True Sight is thematically significant: the person
                  who sees through everything building the art of making
                  others unable to see.
                  Jessica's contempt for Jess should never become simple
                  cruelty. It is a specific kind of contempt — the kind
                  that comes from watching someone capable of more choose
                  to be less. That distinction matters for how she reads
                  on the page.
