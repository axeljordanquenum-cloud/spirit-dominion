================================================================================
                      TALENT SYSTEM — SPIRIT DOMINION
================================================================================

OVERVIEW
--------
A Talent is an innate ability awakened during the Ascension event. Every
individual who survives the process receives one. It is not learned, not
trained — it is simply there, and the individual instinctively knows how to
use it from the moment it activates.

A Talent's apparent strength at first glance is often misleading. Even a
seemingly weak Talent, properly understood and applied, can produce
extraordinary results. The ceiling is rarely visible at the start.

Talents range across every category: combat, utility, perception, crafting,
leadership, and beyond. They are as varied as the people who carry them.

================================================================================
SPIRIT FIGHTERS
================================================================================

WHAT IS A SPIRIT
----------------
A spirit is an entity native to the Hyper-Dimensional Spirit World — a layer
of existence parallel to the physical. Spirits exist in all forms and shapes,
each unique. They are not tools. They are living beings.

A spirit can cross the boundary between worlds and bond with a physical
individual. This bond is symbiotic:
  - The spirit grants the individual access to its power.
  - The individual's battles, growth, and experiences feed the spirit,
    allowing it to grow stronger in return.

The bond is permanent once formed. A spirit cannot be taken, transferred,
or destroyed without consequence to its host.

SPIRIT FIGHTERS
---------------
A spirit fighter is an individual who has bonded with a spirit. The class
exists across many civilizations within the System Multiverse — some
entire worlds have built their power structure around it.

In Universe 711, a newly integrated world, the class is not foreign to the
System but is largely unknown to its inhabitants at the start of the
Ascension. Knowledge of spirits, their nature, and how to develop them
will be something Jay and others must discover and piece together.

The power of a spirit fighter varies enormously and is directly constrained
by one thing: the quality of the spirit they receive.

A weak spirit = a weak spirit fighter, regardless of the host's personal
strength or effort. This is the fundamental ceiling of the class and why,
despite not being rare, most spirit fighters never reach their potential.

A strong spirit = a different story entirely.

================================================================================
JAY'S TALENT — PRIMAL SPIRIT ORB
================================================================================

CLASSIFICATION  : Growth / Luck / Spirit
TYPE            : Active (with passive components)
RARITY          : Singular — no known equivalent in Universe 711

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
CORE ABILITY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
At each rank threshold reached, Jay automatically receives a new innate
spirit. This spirit is born at the moment of rank-up and is unique to that
rank and to Jay's internal state at the time.

Most spirit fighters bond with one spirit across their entire life. The
quality of that spirit defines their ceiling forever.

Jay receives a new spirit at every rank. His ceiling does not exist in the
same way.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SPIRIT NATURE — REFLECTION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Each spirit born from the Primal Spirit Orb is not random. It reflects Jay's
mental and emotional state at the moment of rank-up — his dominant mindset,
his unresolved tensions, his current understanding of himself and the world.

This means:
  - The spirits are deeply personal. No two will be identical across hosts,
    even if such a talent existed in another individual.
  - Jay's inner life directly shapes his power. Growth as a person is
    inseparable from growth as a fighter.
  - A spirit born during a moment of clarity will differ entirely from one
    born during grief, rage, or cold ambition.

The spirits are fixed once born — they do not change their fundamental
nature. But they can grow, evolve, and expand their capabilities through
the bond with Jay.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SPIRIT SCOPE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Each spirit operates across three domains:

  COMBAT    : Direct offensive and defensive applications in battle.
              Each spirit's combat expression is unique to its nature.

  UTILITY   : Non-combat functions — scouting, analysis, environmental
              interaction, support roles within Jay's faction.

  PASSIVE   : Persistent low-level effects that remain active as long as
              the bond is maintained. These may enhance Jay's base stats,
              perception, or provide subtle advantages in specific contexts.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
ACTIVE LIMITATION — THE BUFFER
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The Primal Spirit Orb is an active talent. Jay cannot run at full power
indefinitely.

Summoning and maintaining spirits draws on his internal energy. The more
spirits active simultaneously, the higher the drain. This creates a natural
buffer — a ceiling on how long and how hard Jay can push in any given
engagement.

Early in the story, with only one or two spirits, this limitation is
manageable but present. As his collection grows across ranks, managing
which spirits to deploy and when becomes a strategic layer in itself.

Full power is a finite state, not a default one.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
ADDITIONAL BENEFITS — PRIMAL SPIRIT ORB
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Beyond the core ability, the Primal Spirit Orb grants Jay four permanent
advantages that compound across the entire arc of his growth:

  1. STRENGTHENED SPIRIT ENERGY AFFINITY
     Maintaining an active spirit bond requires spirit energy — a resource
     most spirit fighters struggle to manage. Jay's affinity is innately
     enhanced, reducing the baseline cost of maintaining bonds and making
     him structurally more efficient than other spirit fighters at all stages.
     Additionally, every ability granted by his spirits benefits from:
       - Reduced activation cost
       - Enhanced effect on use
     The same ability in Jay's hands hits harder and costs less than in
     anyone else's.

  2. ENHANCED AFFINITY WITH SPIRIT CREATURES
     Jay's baseline relationship with spirit-type entities is elevated.
     Spirit creatures are naturally less hostile toward him, more responsive
     to interaction, and easier to influence. This has combat applications
     but extends into utility and diplomacy with spirit-aligned beings.

  3. SPIRIT SYNERGY  [ Passive ]
     For each bonded spirit, Jay passively receives:
       - 50% of the spirit's primary attribute
       - 30% of the spirit's secondary attribute
       - 2 random skills from the spirit's ability pool
     This passive scales directly with the number and quality of his spirits.
     A single strong spirit already makes this significant. Across multiple
     ranks, with multiple spirits each growing in power, the cumulative
     stat and skill absorption becomes one of Jay's most decisive edges.

  4. ORIGIN INFUSION  [ Passive ]
     Spirits housed within the Primal Spirit Orb are gradually infused with
     Origin Energy over time. This slow, automatic process reduces the cost
     of spirit evolution — the threshold Jay needs to reach to push a spirit
     to its next form is consistently lower than it would be for any other
     spirit fighter.
     Over a long timeline, this means Jay's spirits evolve faster, cheaper,
     and more frequently than anyone else's. The compounding effect across
     multiple spirits across multiple ranks is difficult to overstate.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SPIRIT EVOLUTION & FUSION — FUTURE MECHANICS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
As Jay grows and the bond deepens, spirits can evolve beyond their initial
form. Evolution is triggered by significant experiences, battles, or
internal shifts — not by time alone.

At a later stage, through acquired skills or abilities, Jay will gain the
capacity to:
  - Fuse two spirits into a single more powerful entity.
  - Deploy spirits in combined or coordinated configurations.
  - Unlock alternative modes of use beyond standard summoning.

These mechanics are locked behind progression and will emerge naturally
as the story advances. They are not available at the start.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
VISIBILITY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Spirits manifested by Jay are visible to others when actively deployed.
Their appearance reflects their nature — shaped by what they represent
within Jay, expressed in physical form.

Jay can choose whether or not to summon them. An unsummoned spirit is
invisible and undetectable by outside observers. This gives Jay a degree
of strategic concealment — his full arsenal is never immediately apparent.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
EARLY STORY STATE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
At the start of Book 1, Arc 1:
  - Jay has just ranked up for the first time during the Ascension.
  - His first spirit is newborn — weak, raw, and largely untested.
  - He instinctively understands the bond but has no framework or
    knowledge to contextualize what he has.
  - To anyone observing, he is simply a spirit fighter with a weak
    starting spirit. Unremarkable on the surface.
  - The true nature and rarity of his Talent is not yet apparent —
    to others, or fully to himself.

This gap between perception and reality is one of the early story's
most important tensions.

================================================================================
DESIGN NOTES
================================================================================

  - The talent is intentionally understated at first glance. "Another
    spirit fighter" is the outside read. The depth only reveals itself
    over time and ranks.

  - The reflection mechanic ties Jay's power system directly to his
    character arc. His internal growth is never decorative — it has
    mechanical consequences.

  - The buffer/limitation prevents the talent from being a win button.
    Jay must think, manage, and choose. The talent rewards intelligence,
    which matches his established character.

  - Spirit fighter knowledge in Universe 711 starts at near zero. Jay
    will have to discover, experiment, and build his understanding from
    scratch — no guides, no mentors at the start.
