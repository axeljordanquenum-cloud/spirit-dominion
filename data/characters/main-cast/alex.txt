TALENT 1: SOUL FORGE
Classification  : Craft / Technique — Creation / Combat hybrid
Timing          : Composite

CORE:
  Alex carries an internal forge — not a physical object but a space within
  him where soul fire burns. The fire is primordial in quality and
  patient in nature. It does not rush. It waits for the right material
  and the right moment, then it works.

POWER ROAD:
  SOUL FIRE      The base expression. Alex can externalize soul fire
                 as a combat element — it is not standard fire magic.
                 It burns differently. Conventional fire suppression
                 has limited effect on it. At early ranks its output
                 is modest. It grows with the quality of what feeds
                 the forge.

  SMELTING       The forge does not just burn. It processes. Alex can
                 smelt the souls of slain opponents — extracting from
                 them something essential that becomes material for
                 his craft. The quality and nature of what he extracts
                 depends entirely on what the opponent was:
                 their rank, their nature, and how they died.
                 A monster's soul yields different materials than a
                 warrior's. A warrior's yields different materials
                 than something older and stranger.

  FORGING        What he makes carries properties that standard
                 crafting cannot produce. His weapons are not
                 enhanced equipment — they are made from things
                 that used to be alive. That distinction matters
                 in how they behave. A weapon forged from strong
                 soul material has properties that align with the
                 source. It is not just powerful. It is specific.

  COMBAT SMITH   Alex is not a support character who occasionally
                 fights. He is a warmaster. The hammer he carries
                 and the soul fire he commands make him one of the
                 faction's most dangerous close-range combatants.
                 The forge and the fight are the same thing in
                 his hands — what he does to opponents in battle
                 feeds what he can build afterward.

  LATE EXPRESSION Over the long arc, what Alex can smelt and
                  what he can forge expands beyond conventional
                  soul material. The implications of what the
                  forge is capable of at high ranks are TBD —
                  but the direction is clear. He is not just
                  building weapons. He is building things that
                  have no category yet.

TALENT 2: TWIN SOUL BASTION
Classification  : Physical Enhancement / Defensive — shared talent
Timing          : Composite
Shared with     : Lexie

CORE:
  See Lexie's character card for full mechanics. Alex carries the same
  half of the shared talent. Key expressions from his perspective:

  MENTAL FORTRESS  Natural resistance to mental attack. Passive,
                   always active, strengthened by proximity to Lexie.

  DAMAGE TRANSFER  When Lexie takes a threshold blow, Alex feels it
                   and absorbs half. When Alex takes a threshold blow,
                   Lexie absorbs half. He cannot prevent this.
                   He can only be aware of it.

  PROXIMITY BOND   His combat output is measurably higher when Lexie
                   is in range. Not by his own effort — the talent
                   amplifies what both of them are doing in proximity.

  COST             The distribution of a threshold blow means Alex
                   takes serious damage when Lexie would take fatal
                   damage. He has made his peace with this. Lexie
                   has not entirely made her peace with his version
                   of peace with it.

[ CHARACTER ARC ]
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Start State     : A man who knows exactly what he is and has not yet
                  been asked to reckon with the full cost of being it.
                  The forge is there. The fire is ready. The question
                  of what it means to consume souls for materials has
                  not been fully opened yet.

Internal Goal   : Make things worthy of what went into them.
                  This is both a craft principle and an unexamined
                  moral position. Eventually the story will ask
                  him to examine it.

External Goal   : Arm the faction for what is coming. Become
                  the warmaster the faction needs at scale.
                  Push the Soul Forge until he understands everything
                  it can do.

Turning Point   : The moment the forge produces something that makes
                  the soul-smelting question impossible to avoid —
                  either because of what he smelted, or because of
                  what the weapon does to someone, or because
                  someone asks him directly and he does not have
                  an answer he can stand behind. TBD.

End State       : TBD — open. The Soul Smith at full expression is
                  someone who makes things that do not exist in any
                  other forge. What those things are and what they
                  cost is the arc.

Theme/Lesson    : Making something powerful is not the same as
                  making something right. The fire does not care
                  about the difference. The smith has to.
