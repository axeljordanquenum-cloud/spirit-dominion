================================================================================
                    TALENT SYSTEM — FULL REFERENCE
                    Spirit Dominion | The System Era
================================================================================

OVERVIEW
--------
A Talent is an innate ability awakened at the moment of Ascension. The name
of the Awakened sublimation stage derives directly from this event — what
wakes at Ascension is the Talent itself.

Not everyone awakens a Talent. Some individuals survive the Ascension process
without awakening anything. This does not exclude them from the path of power
— class, race progression, skills, and bloodline all remain available — but
they operate without the foundational edge that a Talent provides.

A Talent is not a skill. It is not a technique. It is not learned or trained.
It is something that comes naturally to its owner — used as easily as
breathing, requiring no conscious activation overhead. It operates at the
highest order of priority within the System framework and can affect
everything relative to the person who carries it.

KEY PRINCIPLES
--------------
  — No strong or weak Talents in absolute terms. Only well or poorly applied
    ones. A seemingly simple Talent in skilled hands can outperform a
    spectacular one in clumsy ones.

  — Complexity of application varies. Some Talents are immediately obvious
    in their use. Others require deep understanding of their nature to extract
    their full potential.

  — A Talent scales with its owner's power. As the user advances, the Talent
    advances with them — its expressions become more potent, its range wider,
    its ceiling higher.

  — A Talent can transform. At sufficient development, a Talent may evolve
    into something qualitatively different from what it appeared to be at
    Ascension. The category remains fixed — the expression within that
    category can expand dramatically.

  — A Talent is personal. It reflects the nature, potential, and state of
    its owner at the moment of Ascension. Two people cannot carry the same
    Talent in the same form.

================================================================================
AWARENESS & VISIBILITY
================================================================================

OWNER AWARENESS
---------------
The System displays a Talent clearly at the moment of Ascension. The owner
knows what they have from the first instant — name, basic function, and
initial capability are all visible. What they do not immediately know is
the Talent's ceiling, its evolution potential, or the full depth of what
it can become. Discovery is the ongoing process; identification is instant.

VISIBILITY TO OTHERS
--------------------
The existence of a Talent can be sensed by individuals with sufficient
perception, System-assisted reading abilities, or relevant skills. The
sensation is presence — something in the person registers differently to
those capable of reading it.

The nature of the Talent, however, requires deeper reading. Standard
Evaluation Eye reveals that a Talent is present. Understanding what it
actually does demands either a specialized skill, direct observation of
the Talent in use, or a level of spiritual/perceptual attunement that
is uncommon at lower ranks.

Implication: It is possible to know that someone has a Talent without
knowing what it is. This creates meaningful asymmetry in early encounters —
an observer knows they are looking at someone exceptional without knowing
the source or shape of that exceptionality.

================================================================================
MULTIPLE TALENTS
================================================================================
Awakening more than one Talent is a legendary condition. Almost mythic in
the telling — most practitioners will go their entire lives without meeting
someone confirmed to carry two. It is not impossible, but it is so rare
that its existence is disputed in most circles.

When it does occur, both Talents are fully functional and independent. They
do not interfere with each other. The individual carries both at full
capacity. The combination is, by definition, entirely unique to them.

Jay carries one Talent: Primal Spirit Orb. Its singular nature is part of
what defines it — no known equivalent exists in Universe 711.

================================================================================
FOUR CATEGORIES
================================================================================
All Talents belong to one of four categories. The category is fixed at
Ascension and does not change through evolution. The expression within a
category can expand dramatically, but the fundamental nature of what the
Talent is does not cross categorical lines.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
1. PHYSICAL ENHANCEMENT
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Talents that directly augment the body's physical capabilities. Speed,
strength, resilience, regeneration, sensory acuity — anything that enhances
what the physical form can do without introducing an external element.

Characteristics:
  — Immediate tangible benefit. Easy to assess, hard to hide in action.
  — Early strong tendency — physical advantages are relevant at every rank.
  — Ceiling scales with the body's development. As Constitution, Agility,
    and Strength grow, the Talent's amplification produces increasingly
    dramatic results.
  — The simplest category to use. The hardest to use at its absolute ceiling.

Examples:
  — Unbreakable: The owner's physical structure resists fracture beyond
    what any attribute score should allow. Bones that should shatter hold.
    At high ranks, becomes effectively indestructible under conventional force.
  — Second Wind: The owner can push past physical exhaustion thresholds that
    would incapacitate anyone else. Stamina floors are dramatically higher.
    At high ranks, the concept of running out of physical energy becomes
    functionally inapplicable in ordinary combat.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
2. ELEMENTAL / MAGICAL ABILITY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Talents that grant affinity with, control over, or expression of elemental
or magical forces. Fire, water, lightning, space, time, gravity, light,
darkness — the full spectrum of extraordinary phenomena.

Characteristics:
  — Broad range from straightforward (direct elemental control) to complex
    (conceptual manipulation of abstract forces).
  — Scaling is tied to Mystery, Willpower, and the owner's overall rank.
  — Access to elemental affinity through a Talent differs from the standard
    System-granted elemental access at the Extraordinary stage — Talent-based
    elemental control is present from Rank F and tends to run deeper and more
    personal than System-standard access.
  — Late stage tendency for the more conceptual variants. Direct elemental
    control is strong early; manipulation of abstract forces reveals its
    edge only at higher ranks.

Examples:
  — Ember Core: A permanent internal flame the owner can externalize at
    will. Straightforward fire expression — heat, combustion, light.
    At high ranks, the flame begins expressing conceptual properties beyond
    temperature: burning concepts, burning intent, burning memory.
  — Void Step: The owner can briefly occupy the space between spaces —
    a form of short-range spatial displacement. Simple at low ranks.
    At high ranks, the "void" between spaces proves to be something with
    its own rules and contents.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
3. SKILL, WEAPON & COMBAT MASTERY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Talents that enhance the owner's capacity to learn, execute, or excel in
combat-related domains. Weapon mastery, technique acquisition speed, combat
reading, reflex enhancement, fighting instinct — anything that makes the
owner a more effective combatant through mastery rather than raw physical
or magical power.

Characteristics:
  — The most immediately applicable category in a combat-heavy early arc.
  — Composite tendency — strong early (rapid skill acquisition, combat edge)
    and scales well (mastery depth increases as the owner advances).
  — Often overlooked in favor of flashier Talents. Consistently
    underestimated until its owner has had time to accumulate skill depth.
  — The ceiling is less obvious than physical or elemental Talents but
    no less real. A master combatant with deep technique mastery at high
    ranks operates differently from a powerful one — precision and
    adaptability replace raw output as the primary expression.

Examples:
  — Perfect Mimicry: On witnessing a technique once, the owner can reproduce
    it at a functional level immediately. Not instant mastery — but the
    foundation is laid in a single observation. At high ranks, the
    reproduction approaches the original's ceiling rather than its floor.
  — Blade Sense: Absolute spatial awareness of any bladed weapon in range —
    trajectories, timing, openings. At low ranks, makes the owner
    extraordinarily difficult to cut. At high ranks, extends to all
    weapons, then to all attacks, then to intent.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
4. CRAFT / TECHNIQUE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Talents that enhance the owner's capacity to create, build, analyze, or
refine. Crafting, alchemy, formation-setting, inscription, analysis of
enemy techniques, tactical perception — anything that operates through
the application of knowledge, skill, and process rather than direct
physical or magical action.

Characteristics:
  — The most misunderstood category in terms of combat relevance.
    Underestimated consistently by those who equate combat power with
    direct output.
  — Late stage tendency for most craft Talents — their advantage is
    cumulative and compounding. Early game they provide utility; late
    game they provide leverage that direct-combat Talents cannot replicate.
  — The ceiling is effectively open-ended. A crafter or analyst operating
    at the peak of their Talent at high ranks reshapes entire conflicts
    from behind the line of engagement.
  — Composite variants in this category are particularly dangerous —
    a Talent that combines early utility with late-stage compounding
    leverage is one of the most strategically potent configurations possible.

Examples:
  — Perfect Recall: The owner retains everything they observe or experience
    with total fidelity. No degradation, no misremembering. At low ranks,
    a learning and analysis edge. At high ranks, the recalled experiences
    include the sensory and physical memory of techniques — recall becomes
    a form of technique library with perfect execution templates.
  — Inscription Prodigy: Formation-setting and inscription work that would
    take a master months completes in days. At low ranks, the owner
    produces functional formations rapidly. At high ranks, the inscriptions
    begin reflecting the owner's intent in ways the standard formation
    vocabulary cannot contain — new formations that have no name yet.

================================================================================
TIMING ARCHETYPES
================================================================================

EARLY STRONG
------------
Talents that grant powerful, directly applicable abilities from the moment
of Ascension. Their value is immediate and obvious. The owner enters every
early engagement with a clear edge.

Characteristics:
  — Straightforward application — the Talent's use is intuitive from
    the start.
  — Scaling is linear or moderate — the Talent remains useful throughout
    but does not accelerate dramatically at high ranks.
  — The ceiling is visible early. What you see at Rank F is a smaller
    version of what exists at Rank A, not a qualitatively different thing.

Role in story: Common among antagonists and early rivals. The person with
an Early Strong Talent is formidable now. The person with a Composite or
Late Stage Talent will surpass them — but that takes time, and in the
early game, time is often not available.

LATE STAGE
----------
Talents whose edge is not fully apparent at Ascension. Early on, the Talent
may appear modest, limited, or situationally useful. The ceiling is not
visible from the start — it reveals itself only as the owner advances.

Characteristics:
  — Complex application — the full depth of the Talent requires understanding
    that the owner must develop over time.
  — Scaling is non-linear — the Talent accelerates at higher ranks,
    producing advantages that early-game observers could not have predicted
    from the initial presentation.
  — The most dangerous Talents to underestimate. The owner often appears
    weaker than their peers in the early arc — until they don't.

Role in story: Common among figures who define later arcs. The gap between
their early presentation and their eventual ceiling is a narrative engine
in itself.

COMPOSITE
---------
Talents that are strong early AND scale disproportionately well with the
owner's advancement. The rarest and most coveted archetype. The owner
enters the early game with a genuine edge and retains it — and widens it
— at every subsequent stage.

Characteristics:
  — Dual expression — meaningful at Rank F, qualitatively different and
    more powerful at Rank C, nearly incomparable at Rank A and beyond.
  — The owner is never in the position of trading present strength for
    future potential. They have both.
  — The ceiling is not only high but changes shape as it rises — what the
    Talent is at Rank SS is not simply a larger version of what it was at
    Rank F. It becomes something different while remaining continuous.

Role in story: The rarest characters. Recognized by those with the knowledge
to see what they are looking at — and deeply dangerous to leave unchecked.

JAY'S TALENT — CLASSIFICATION
------------------------------
Primal Spirit Orb is a Composite Talent in the Spirit / Elemental-adjacent
category (cross-categorical in nature — classified under Elemental / Magical
for System purposes given its extraordinary energy basis).

  Early expression  : One newborn spirit at Rank F. Appears modest. Most
                      observers see a spirit fighter with a weak starting
                      spirit. Unremarkable on the surface.
  Late expression   : Seven spirits at Rank S, all compounding through
                      Spirit Synergy, fusion stages, and Origin Infusion.
                      The Talent that started everything becomes the seventh
                      and final bond.
  SS transformation : The Talent ceases to be a discrete ability and becomes
                      the origin state of Jay's existence. Origin Spirit.

The gap between the early presentation and the eventual ceiling is the
central dramatic engine of Jay's arc.

================================================================================
TALENT & CLASS INTERACTION
================================================================================
A Talent operates above and beyond the class system. It is not granted by
a class, not locked behind class quality requirements, and cannot be
suppressed by class-based restrictions.

However, the interaction between Talent and class is where individual power
expression becomes truly unique:

  — A Talent can enhance class skills beyond their normal ceiling.
  — A class built around a Talent's nature produces synergies unavailable
    to any other combination.
  — The System recognizes deep Talent-class alignment and can produce
    unique class names and evolutions that reflect both.

Jay's Spirit Fighter class is the direct product of this synthesis — a class
that would not exist without the Primal Spirit Orb, and a Talent that would
not reach its ceiling without a class built to support it.

================================================================================
TALENT & BLOODLINE INTERACTION
================================================================================
Bloodline is a separate power source from Talent. They do not interfere
with each other and both operate simultaneously.

A high-quality bloodline can influence which Talent awakens at Ascension
by shaping the individual's nature and potential — but the Talent itself,
once awakened, is independent of the bloodline.

Rare bloodlines may carry inherited Talents — predispositions toward
specific Talent types that are passed generation to generation. These are
not guaranteed awakening but significantly increase the probability.
