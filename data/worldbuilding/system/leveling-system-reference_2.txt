OVERVIEW
--------
Every integrated entity operates on two parallel progression tracks:

  CLASS TRACK   — Skills, combat role, technique-based advancement.
                  Advances through class-aligned actions and kills.
                  Awards auto-attribute points + small FA bonus per level.

  RACE TRACK    — Biological and metaphysical sublimation of the body.
                  Primary source of Free Attribute Points (FA).
                  Advances through kills (reduced rate) + non-kill sources.

Both tracks feed from the same kill pool but split the EXP independently.
They can desync. Race level hard-gates certain class rank-ups at critical nodes.

================================================================================
EXP REQUIRED PER LEVEL
================================================================================

Formula (per track, independently):
  EXP(local_lv → local_lv+1) = local_lv^1.65 × C_req

Constants:
  α = 1.65   (fixed across all stages and tracks)
  C_req      (shifts at each sublimation stage boundary — see Stage Transition)

Awakened baseline:
  C_req = 100

Sample requirements (Awakened, C_req = 100):
  Lv 1  → 2   :     100 EXP
  Lv 2  → 3   :     314 EXP
  Lv 3  → 4   :     613 EXP
  Lv 5  → 6   :   1,317 EXP
  Lv 10 → 11  :   4,467 EXP
  Lv 14 → 15  :   7,800 EXP
  Lv 25 → 26  :  19,700 EXP
  Lv 50 → 51  :  63,500 EXP (Awakened overall peak)

NOTE: Each track tracks its own local level and requirement independently.
A character at Class Lv8 + Race Lv5 has two separate requirement counters
running — one at Lv8 for class, one at Lv5 for race.

================================================================================
KILL EXP — FULL FORMULA
================================================================================

OVERALL LEVEL
-------------
Kill EXP yield and ratio both use OVERALL LEVEL:
  Overall Lv = attacker's class track level + attacker's race track level
  Same for opponent.

MASTER FORMULA
--------------
  Kill EXP (total pool) = Opp overall Lv^1.35 × C_kill × Rank Factor
                          × (Opp overall Lv / Your overall Lv)

Constants:
  β      = 1.35   (fixed across all stages)
  C_kill = 10     (Awakened baseline — shifts at stage boundaries)

RANK FACTOR
-----------
  Opponent below your rank     :  ×0      Hard zero. No EXP.
  Same rank                    :  ×1.0    Baseline.
  +1 rank (adjacent letter)    :  ×2.0    One boundary crossed.
  +2 ranks                     :  ×3.5    Two boundaries crossed.
  +3 ranks                     :  ×5.0    +1.5 per additional rank, uncapped.
  +4 ranks                     :  ×6.5
  +5 ranks                     :  ×8.0    (continues linearly)

EXP SPLIT — CLASS VS RACE
--------------------------
After total kill pool is calculated, it splits between tracks based on
kill method alignment:

  Alignment tier      Class share   Race share
  ----------------    -----------   ----------
  Fully aligned       60%           40%
  Partially aligned   20%           80%
  Unaligned           0%            100%

  Fully aligned    : Kill achieved through core class method.
  Partially aligned: Hybrid method — class involved but not primary.
  Unaligned        : Kill achieved entirely outside class identity.

Examples:
  Warrior kills with sword         → Fully aligned (60/40)
  Warrior kills with sword + gun   → Partially aligned (20/80)
  Warrior kills with gun only      → Unaligned (0/100)
  Spirit Fighter uses spirit ability → Fully aligned (60/40)
  Spirit Fighter uses a trap       → Partially aligned (20/80)

TEAM EXP SPLIT
--------------
When multiple members contribute to a kill:

  Base EXP : Split by contribution weight (proportional to damage dealt).
  Kill shot : Recipient gets an additional +40% of their contribution share.
             Kill shot bonus does NOT apply to the quality bonus pool.
  Split     : Rank factor applied before split. Each member receives their
             share then routes it 60/40 (or per alignment) independently.

  Quality floor rule:
  The team's quality floor is set by the highest-quality CONTRIBUTING member
  (those who dealt damage). Non-contributing members do not affect the floor.
  Quality bonus only triggers if opponent quality exceeds the floor.

================================================================================
QUALITY BONUS POOL
================================================================================

Awarded as a SEPARATE pool on top of base kill EXP.
Triggered only when opponent's class/bloodline quality exceeds the highest
quality among contributing team members (or the solo attacker).

Both class quality and bloodline quality use the same table.
Bloodline bonus stacks independently on top of class quality bonus.

GAP BONUS (% of total base kill EXP — post rank factor):
  +1 quality tier   :  +45%
  +2 quality tiers  :  +110%
  +3 quality tiers  :  +200%
  +4 quality tiers  :  +320%
  +5 quality tiers  :  +475%
  +6 tiers+         :  +475% + 180% per additional tier

FLAT UNLOCK (scaled by opponent overall level):
  Flat bonus = base flat × (opponent overall Lv / 10)

  Opponent quality   Base flat
  ----------------   ---------
  Elite              70
  Rare               175
  Epic               470
  Apex               1,170
  Legendary          2,920
  Celestial+         +1,750 per tier

Quality pool splits by contribution weight (same as base EXP).
Kill shot bonus does NOT apply to the quality pool.

EXP MODIFIERS — SKILLS / TITLES / TALENTS
================================================================================

All modifiers are ADDITIVE within each axis.
A single source can affect both EXP requirements and kill yield simultaneously.

SOURCE RANGES:
  Source    EXP req reduction   Kill yield boost   Gate
  -------   -----------------   ----------------   --------------------
  Skill     2% – 8%             5% – 15%           Level threshold
  Title     5% – 20%            10% – 30%          Achievement
  Talent    10% – 35%           15% – 50%          Innate / progressive

SOFT CAP — EXP REQUIREMENT REDUCTION:
  0% – 30%   : Full effect. Every % applies at face value.
  30% – 60%  : Half efficiency. Each additional % costs 2% of modifier value.
  60%+       : Hard ceiling. No further reduction regardless of modifiers.

Kill yield boost has NO hard cap. Stacks additively without ceiling.

APPLICATION ORDER:
  Quality coefficient → Rank coefficient → Skills/Titles/Talents → Potential

================================================================================
NON-KILL EXP SOURCES
================================================================================

Non-kill actions generate EXP at reduced efficiency. Diminishing returns
apply to repeated actions — the System rewards genuine novelty and effort.
Farming the same action yields progressively less.

  Source                    Efficiency vs kills   Track routing       Split
  -----------------------   -------------------   -----------------   --------
  Crafting / non-combat     50% – 70%             Class only          100/0
  Faction / leadership      30% – 50%             Split               35/65
  Training / practice       20% – 35%             Class only          100/0
  Exploration / discovery   10% – 20%             Race only           0/100

  Crafting / non-combat : Rewards doing what your class is built for.
                          Crafter and specialist classes can progress
                          meaningfully without combat.
  Faction / leadership  : 35% class / 65% race. Authority shapes the
                          being more than the class.
  Training / practice   : Reinforces class identity. No race benefit —
                          the body needs real stress, not repetition,
                          to sublimate.
  Exploration/discovery : Exposure feeds the race track. New environments
                          and self-understanding accelerate sublimation.

================================================================================
NARRATIVE GATES
================================================================================

Gates activate at rank threshold levels (the final level before rank-up).
They filter which actions generate EXP — they do not freeze progression.

GATE MECHANIC:
  Qualifying action     → Full EXP (all modifiers apply normally)
  Non-qualifying action → EXP × 0.05 (5% of normal yield)

GATE CONDITIONS:
  Base condition set by class identity.
  Achievement history can modify it or add additional valid conditions.
  Multiple valid conditions can exist simultaneously — any one qualifies.

  Condition type        Source         Description
  ------------------    -----------    ------------------------------------------
  Solo combat           Class base     Only solo kills qualify.
  Over-rank kill        Class base     Only kills against higher rank qualify.
  Over-quality kill     Class base     Only kills against higher quality qualify.
  Achievement unlock    History mod    Past feat adds a valid condition.
  Discovery unlock      History mod    Self-understanding adds a valid condition.

GATE LEVELS (rank threshold):
  Rank F → E : Level 10 (class track)
  Rank E → D : Level 25 (class track)
  Rank D → C : Level 50 (class track)
  (Continues at each rank ceiling)

================================================================================
FREE ATTRIBUTE POINTS (FA)
================================================================================

FA comes from two sources with different roles:

RACE TRACK — PRIMARY FA SOURCE:
  Bulk FA awarded per race level-up. Manually allocated by the character.
  This is where build identity and attribute customization live.

  Sublimation stage   FA per race level-up
  -----------------   --------------------
  Awakened            15 – 25
  Extraordinary       30 – 50
  Named               60 – 100
  Eternal             TBD

CLASS TRACK — SECONDARY FA + AUTO-ATTRIBUTES:
  Small FA bonus awarded per class level-up.
  Auto-attribute points also awarded — weighted ~70% toward primary class
  attributes, ~30% spread across secondary. Not manually allocated.

  Class quality   Rank F FA/lv   Rank E FA/lv   Rank D FA/lv
  -------------   ------------   ------------   ------------
  Basic           1 – 2          2 – 3          3 – 5
  Elite           2 – 3          3 – 5          5 – 7
  Rare            3 – 5          5 – 8          7 – 11
  Epic            5 – 7          7 – 11         10 – 15
  Apex            7 – 10         10 – 15        14 – 20

DIMINISHING RETURNS:
  FA allocation cost grows as an attribute's total rises.
  Early points in an attribute are efficient.
  Later points cost proportionally more. No hard ceiling — only increasing
  price. Rewards focused builds over extreme min-maxing.

  FA allocation takes time to absorb. Effect builds progressively.
  Regional weighting: areas most actively used after allocation gain more.

SKILL UNLOCK THRESHOLDS
================================================================================

Threshold positions shift with class quality.
Quality also determines what fills the slot — higher quality class unlocks
higher quality skill at the same threshold.

RANK F (10 class levels):
  Class Lv 5   : First skill slot. Basic quality. Reflects early state.
  Class Lv 8   : First slot upgrades to Elite quality (Elite+ class only).
  Class Lv 10  : Second skill slot. Narrative gate active.
                 Requires meaningful combat to unlock.
  Class Lv 13  : Second slot upgrades to Elite quality (Elite+ class only).
  Class Lv 15  : Rank-up gate + class advancement check.
                 Class quality minimum enforced.

RANK E (15 class levels):
  Class Lv 5   : Third skill slot. First new slot after rank-up.
  Class Lv 10  : Slot upgrade window. Elite+ can upgrade any existing slot.
  Class Lv 15  : Fourth skill slot. Rare+ unlocks higher quality slot.
  Class Lv 20  : Second upgrade window. Narrative gate active.
  Class Lv 25  : Rank-up gate + class advancement check.
                 Rare quality minimum enforced for Rank D.

================================================================================
DESYNC — RACE VS CLASS HARD GATES
================================================================================

Race level must meet minimum thresholds at critical class rank-up nodes.

  Class rank-up   Required race milestone         Consequence if unmet
  -------------   ----------------------------    ----------------------------
  F → E           Awakened stage active           Class rank-up hard blocked.
  E → D           Awakened — mid progression      Threshold TBD.
  D → C           Extraordinary stage required    Hard block. Cannot enter
                                                  Rank C without Extraordinary
                                                  sublimation. Non-negotiable.

The D→C gate is structurally the most significant — Rank C represents a
fundamental break from mortal constraints. The race track must reflect that
shift. A character cannot grind class levels past Rank D without their
race track keeping pace.

================================================================================
SUBLIMATION STAGE TRANSITION — FORMULA SCALING
================================================================================

At each sublimation stage boundary, the formula does NOT carry overall level
forward. Instead local level resets to 1 — but C_kill and C_req are
recalibrated so that Lv1 of the new stage opens at the same yield and
requirement weight as the previous stage's peak.

β and α remain fixed forever (1.35 and 1.65).
Only C_kill and C_req shift at each boundary.

TRANSITION FORMULA:
  new_C_kill = old_C_kill × (stage_entry_overall_lv + 1)^β   [uses opponent overall lv]
  new_C_req  = old_C_req  × (track_peak_lv + 1)^α            [uses character track peak lv]

C_kill uses overall level because it concerns the opponent — overall level
is always the relevant axis for yield calculation.
C_req uses the character's own track peak local level — each track computes
its own C_req independently since class and race advance separately.

AWAKENED → EXTRAORDINARY EXAMPLE:
  Awakened peak overall lv (opponent axis) = 100 (50 class + 50 race)
  Awakened peak track local lv (character axis) = 50 (per track)
  C_kill (Awakened) = 10
  C_req  (Awakened) = 100

  new_C_kill = 10 × (100+1)^1.35 = 10 × 101^1.35
  101^1.35 : ln(101)×1.35 = 4.615×1.35 = 6.230, e^6.230 = 507
  new_C_kill = 10 × 507 = 5,070

  new_C_req = 100 × (50+1)^1.65 = 100 × 51^1.65
  51^1.65 : ln(51)×1.65 = 3.932×1.65 = 6.488, e^6.488 = 659
  new_C_req = 100 × 659 = 65,900

Verify — Lv1 of Extraordinary (class track):
  Kill yield : 1^1.35 × 5,070 = 5,070 EXP
  vs. continuous overall Lv101 : 101^1.35 × 10 = 507 × 10 = 5,070 ✓

  EXP req   : 1^1.65 × 65,900 = 65,900 EXP
  vs. continuous track Lv51 : 51^1.65 × 100 = 659 × 100 = 65,900 ✓

The formula produces output identical to the continuous curve at the boundary,
then scales naturally upward from that floor across the new stage.
