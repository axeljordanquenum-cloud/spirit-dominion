================================================================================
                    ACHIEVEMENT & TITLE SYSTEM
                    Spirit Dominion | The System Era
================================================================================

OVERVIEW
--------
The System does not forget. Every action an integrated entity takes, every
threshold crossed, every first and every extreme — the System records it all
in a permanent log called the Achievement Register. Achievements do not expire,
cannot be revoked, and accumulate across a lifetime.

Titles are a separate layer. They are not the achievements themselves — they
are what the System grants in recognition of them. A Title is earned, then
held in the character's Title Pool. From that pool, a limited number can be
equipped at any time. Only equipped Titles are active.

This creates a meaningful management layer as a character's history deepens:
a growing pool of earned recognition, a hard limit on what can be worn at
once, and an ongoing question of which part of who you are is most useful
right now.

The distinction matters:

  ACHIEVEMENT REGISTER : Permanent. Always visible. The full record.
                         Cannot be equipped, traded, or lost.

  TITLE POOL           : All Titles earned. Grows over time.
                         Only a subset can be active at once.

  EQUIPPED TITLES      : Active Titles. Their effects are live.
                         Switching is allowed but not instant — see below.

================================================================================
ACHIEVEMENT REGISTER
================================================================================

WHAT QUALIFIES
--------------
The System awards achievements for actions it judges to be significant within
the context of the individual's history and the world they inhabit. The
specific conditions are never displayed in advance. Characters discover what
the System considers worth recording by living and doing — not by consulting
a checklist.

General categories the System tracks:

  FIRSTS               The first time something is done. First kill. First
                       rank-up. First spirit bond. First faction member.
                       First time a character does something no one in their
                       world has done before.

  EXTREMES             Actions at the edge of what is measurably possible —
                       killing far above rank, surviving impossible odds,
                       completing a challenge in conditions no one was
                       meant to complete it in.

  ACCUMULATION         Volume thresholds crossed. 100 kills. 1,000 kills.
                       A certain amount of damage dealt. A certain number of
                       distinct opponents defeated.

  NATURE               What a character is, expressed consistently enough
                       that the System recognizes it as identity rather than
                       incident. Someone who has killed a hundred times without
                       allies is not just a fighter — the System may name what
                       that actually is.

  SOCIAL / FACTION     Building something. Leading it. Keeping it alive.
                       Making agreements that hold. Being recognized as
                       authority by other integrated entities.

  DISCOVERY            Finding things the System considers worth noting —
                       locations, entities, mechanics, phenomena.

HOW IT APPEARS IN THE INTERFACE
--------------------------------
The Achievement Register is a scrollable log in the System interface.
Each entry shows:

  — A name (always present)
  — A date/timestamp relative to the Ascension event
  — A brief record of what was done
  — Whether a Title was awarded and which one

The register is visible to the character at all times. It is not visible to
others unless the character chooses to display it — and even then, only
entries the character selects can be shared. The full log is private.

================================================================================
TITLES
================================================================================

WHAT A TITLE IS
---------------
A Title is the System's named recognition of something a character has done
or become. It is permanent once awarded — it cannot be lost, revoked, or
transferred. Even if the character never equips it, it exists in their pool.

Every Title has four components:

  1. NAME           : The label the System assigns. Chosen by the System's
                      own judgment — often evocative, sometimes cryptic.
                      High-rarity Titles tend to name things directly.
                      Lower-rarity Titles are more descriptive.

  2. FLAVOR TEXT    : A line or two. Not mechanical description — this is
                      the System speaking as a recorder. It reads as
                      annotation on a life: what was done, what it meant,
                      what it says about who this person is.

  3. EFFECTS        : The mechanical benefits. What changes when the Title
                      is equipped. These are always visible on inspection.

  4. RARITY         : The System's classification of how exceptional the
                      achievement was. Affects presentation, effect strength,
                      and in some cases, what becomes possible while it is worn.

TITLE RARITY TIERS
------------------
Titles follow a rarity ladder. The tier reflects how extraordinary the
achievement was — relative to the world the character inhabits, the rank
they were operating at, and the conditions involved.

  COMMON
  ------
  The foundation. These titles cover the expected milestones of any
  integrated entity's early development. First kills. First rank-up.
  Standard accumulation thresholds.
  
  Presentation: A clean System notification, no ceremony. The title
  appears in the pool. The character can inspect it at leisure.
  
  Effects: Minor. Usually a small EXP yield boost (5–10%) or a flat
  bonus to one attribute. Not game-changing but not trivial — they
  accumulate across a career and the early ones stick around longest.

  UNCOMMON
  ---------
  Achievements that most integrated entities will reach eventually but
  not trivially. Things that require real effort, meaningful encounters,
  or demonstrated consistency over time.
  
  Presentation: A brief notification with more substance — the System
  names the deed as well as the title. Slightly more weight.
  
  Effects: Meaningful. EXP bonuses in the 10–15% range. Attribute
  bonuses that begin to matter. First appearances of social effects.

  RARE
  -----
  Things most integrated entities will not achieve. Either the conditions
  are unusual, the standard is high, or the action required genuine
  capability beyond typical rank.
  
  Presentation: The System pauses. Not dramatically — but there is a
  noticeable beat before the notification arrives. The phrasing shifts.
  The System does not just record. It acknowledges.
  
  Effects: Significant. EXP bonuses of 15–20%. Substantial attribute
  bonuses. Social effects that carry weight in interactions with other
  System entities. Possible narrative gates.

  EPIC
  -----
  Achievements that mark a character as exceptional among their
  contemporaries at any rank. Things most people in their generation
  will not do.
  
  Presentation: The System interface changes momentarily. Not violently —
  but the visual signature of the notification is distinct. The character
  understands they have done something the System has not seen often.
  
  Effects: High EXP modifiers (20–25%). Multi-attribute bonuses. Social
  effects that open doors and close others. Narrative gates that shift
  how the world responds to this character's presence.

  LEGENDARY
  ----------
  The rarest tier in normal progression. These titles mark events the
  System considers historic — not just rare but significant at a
  civilizational or world-historical level. Many integrated entities
  live their entire lives without earning one.
  
  Presentation: Unmistakable. The System's notification for a Legendary
  Title is not quiet. The character knows. In some cases, others nearby
  may sense that the System has recognized something — though they cannot
  read what was awarded.
  
  Effects: The top of the modifier range (25–30% EXP yield boost and up).
  Major attribute bonuses. Social effects that are felt on sight — entities
  that interact with the System can register the weight of a Legendary
  Title through proximity alone. Narrative gates that are unavailable at
  any lower tier.

  UNIQUE
  -------
  Beyond the standard rarity ladder. Unique Titles are singular — one
  holder across the entire history of the System in Universe 711. They
  are awarded for deeds so specific to a character's nature, path, and
  moment that no general criterion could have predicted them.

  Unique Titles are not just records. They are, in some cases, names —
  the System naming what a person actually is. The distinction between
  a Unique Title and a True Name is not always clear, even to the System.
  
  Presentation: Not a notification. Something more like a statement.
  Some characters report the experience as the System speaking directly —
  not as an interface but as a presence.
  
  Effects: Variable and specific to the title. Not constrained by
  standard modifier tables. Some Unique Titles have effects that
  exist in no other category.

================================================================================
TITLE EFFECTS — CATEGORIES
================================================================================

All Titles draw their effects from these categories. Most Titles combine
two or three. Higher rarity Titles tend to combine more and hit harder
within each.

EXP MODIFIERS
  Already established in the leveling system. Titles contribute 5–30%
  EXP req reduction and 10–30% kill yield boost. These are additive
  with Skill and Talent modifiers. (Soft cap on req reduction: 30% full
  efficiency, 60% hard ceiling. Kill yield boost uncapped.)

ATTRIBUTE BONUSES
  Flat bonuses to one or more attributes. These are always-on while the
  Title is equipped. They do not interact with the diminishing returns
  system — they are additive to the total but applied before soft cap
  calculations.

  Common Titles: +1 to +3 in a single attribute.
  Uncommon:      +3 to +8 across one or two attributes.
  Rare:          +8 to +20 across two or three attributes.
  Epic:          +20 to +50 across multiple attributes.
  Legendary:     +50 and above. Some Legendary Titles apply a percentage
                 boost rather than a flat value.

SOCIAL / FACTION EFFECTS
  The Title's record creates tangible effects in interactions with the
  System and with entities who can read System status:

  — Intimidation weight: Some Titles carry an implicit record that other
    entities register in their System read of the holder. A Title earned
    by killing far above rank announces something about this person before
    they speak.

  — Reputation signals: Faction leadership, diplomatic, and civic Titles
    create recognition effects — the holder is identified as a known
    quantity in relevant circles.

  — Recruitment effects: Certain Titles increase the likelihood that
    unaffiliated entities will approach rather than avoid. Faction-related.

  — Enemy response: Titles that mark a character as dangerous may cause
    weaker entities to hesitate, flee, or defer without engagement.

NARRATIVE GATES
  Some events in the world will only trigger if the involved party holds
  a qualifying Title. This is not widely understood or exploitable —
  the conditions are not published. But the System uses Title history
  as one of its filters for what becomes accessible:

  — Certain quests will not appear in the interface without the right Title.
  — Certain entities will not speak plainly, offer information, or engage
    at depth with someone who has not demonstrated something the System
    considers prerequisite.
  — Some structural advancement opportunities — class challenges, faction
    recognition, access to rare locations — are gated behind specific
    Title criteria.

================================================================================
EQUIPPED TITLE SLOTS
================================================================================

A character can only benefit from a limited number of Titles simultaneously.
Unequipped Titles sit in the pool — real, permanent, but dormant.

SLOT PROGRESSION BY RANK
  Rank F   : 1 slot
  Rank E   : 2 slots
  Rank D   : 3 slots
  Rank C   : 4 slots
  Rank B   : 5 slots
  Rank A   : 6 slots
  Rank S   : 7 slots
  Rank SS  : 8 slots
  Rank Ex  : 10 slots

SWITCHING RULES
  — Switching equipped Titles is possible from the System interface at
    any time outside of active combat.
  — During combat, the current loadout is locked. The System does not
    allow mid-fight swapping.
  — There is no cost to switching outside combat, but effects from
    swapped-out Titles do not persist — they cut immediately.
  — Some Titles have a brief integration period when newly equipped:
    passive attribute bonuses apply instantly; more complex effects
    (social, narrative gate) take a short period to register.

STRATEGIC LAYER
  As a character accumulates Titles across ranks, the decision of which
  to equip becomes a real strategic consideration:

  — A Title optimized for EXP gain is most valuable during leveling pushes.
  — A Title with social effects becomes most valuable during political arcs.
  — A Title that opens narrative gates should be equipped when entering
    contexts where those gates are relevant.
  — The right loadout changes with circumstances. This is intentional.

================================================================================
TITLE PRESENTATION — BY RARITY
================================================================================

The way the System delivers a Title notification is its own signal:

  COMMON     : Simple text entry in the log. "Title earned: [Name]." No pause,
               no ceremony. The character notices if they are watching.
               If they are not, it waits in the log.

  UNCOMMON   : A brief System notification surfaces alongside the log entry.
               The System names what was done: "[Action]. Title awarded: [Name]."
               Slightly more present. Still quiet.

  RARE       : The System pauses the interface for a beat. The notification
               uses the deed and the title together, with the flavor text
               visible immediately. Something in the presentation registers
               as distinct from routine.

  EPIC       : The System interface shifts its visual signature for the
               notification. The character feels the notification differently
               than standard — not painful or overwhelming, but weightier.
               The flavor text reads as deliberate. The System is not
               just recording. It is marking.

  LEGENDARY  : Unmistakable. The notification occupies the full interface
               for a moment. The System speaks the title's flavor text
               before the name — inverting the normal order. The deed
               comes last. As if what was done is already known and the
               System is simply giving it a name that fits.

  UNIQUE     : Outside the normal notification pattern entirely. The
               character's experience varies — some describe a silence
               before the notification arrives, a moment where the System
               feels present rather than passive. The title arrives not
               as a reward but as a recognition. The System has seen
               something it does not have a template for. It made one.

================================================================================
JAY — ASCENSION TITLES
================================================================================

Jay's compressed quest window — 1 hour against the standard 24 — combined
with his performance and the Primal Spirit Orb's activation generated two
Title awards at the moment of Ascension.

The System registered what happened. It named what it saw.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
TITLE 1  |  RARITY: RARE

  Name          : Against the Clock

  Flavor Text   : "Assigned one hour where others were given twenty-four.
                   Completed. The System notes the margin was not close."

  Effects       :
    — EXP requirement reduction: +12% (additive)
    — Agility: +6
    — Narrative gate: In future System quests, time-limited challenge
      conditions are treated as a known quantity — Jay receives a brief
      System acknowledgment when time-gated scenarios are involved.
      Does not reduce the difficulty. Marks him as someone the System
      already knows has operated under pressure.

  Presentation  : Arrived at the end of the Ascension quest completion
                  with a beat before the notification — the System naming
                  the time margin explicitly in the record.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
TITLE 2  |  RARITY: EPIC

  Name          : First of His Kind

  Flavor Text   : "No record of this configuration exists in the System's
                   index for Universe 711. The Primal Spirit Orb has no
                   precedent here. This entity is the first."

  Effects       :
    — Kill EXP yield boost: +20% (additive)
    — Mystery: +12
    — Willpower: +8
    — Social effect: Spirit-type entities register the Title's weight in
      their System read of Jay — not as threat, but as recognition.
      Spirit creatures' baseline disposition toward him is elevated
      (stacks additively with the Primal Spirit Orb's Enhanced Affinity
      with Spirit Creatures benefit).
    — Narrative gate: Certain spirit-world entities and phenomena that
      would not engage with a standard spirit fighter will interact with
      Jay. The Title signals something the System considers significant
      about his nature.

  Presentation  : The System interface shifted for this one. The flavor text
                  arrived before the Title name. Jay understood, at the end
                  of the worst hour of his life, that the System had just
                  told him something true about himself — not as comfort,
                  but as fact.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
EQUIPPED AT ARC 1 START:
  Jay has 1 slot at Rank F. He must choose between Against the Clock
  and First of His Kind. Both sit in his pool. Only one is active.

  This is his first Title decision. The choice is mechanical but also
  a statement about what he thinks he needs most right now.

================================================================================
DESIGN NOTES
================================================================================

  - The Achievement Register is the System playing historian. The full
    record is private — this matters for Jay specifically. What he has
    done, and what the System has noted about it, is not on display.
    Others can sense the weight of equipped Titles but cannot read the log.

  - The single slot at Rank F is a deliberate early constraint. Jay's first
    choice — Against the Clock vs First of His Kind — is worth treating
    as a real character beat. The EXP optimization pick and the identity
    pick are different answers.

  - Title rarity should feel earned in the prose. A Common Title gets a
    sentence. A Legendary Title should stop the scene.

  - The social effects of Titles are intentionally not always positive.
    A Title that announces someone as dangerous draws attention that is
    not always welcome. Jay's Arc 1 Titles are not threatening — but as
    the story escalates, the question of what his record announces about
    him becomes relevant.

  - Unique Titles are the System's version of a True Name. Not the same
    thing — but the boundary blurs at high enough rank. This is intentional
    and worth holding for later arcs.

  - Jess, Alex, Lexie, and Samantha will each generate their own Title
    history through their arcs. Their Ascension Titles (if any) are TBD
    and should be developed with the same specificity as Jay's.
