================================================================================
                    FACTION SYSTEM — FULL REFERENCE
                    Spirit Dominion | The System Era
================================================================================

OVERVIEW
--------
A faction is a System-recognized collective. The moment a faction is
registered, it becomes a distinct entity in the System's record — separate
from the individuals who comprise it, with its own rank, history, and
accumulation of achievements. The System treats it as a thing that exists
in its own right, not merely as a group of people who have agreed to travel
together.

This distinction matters. A faction's rank does not directly reflect the
personal power of its members. It reflects what the faction has done,
what it controls, how many people operate under its banner, and what the
System has chosen to recognize about its place in the world. A faction of
ten Rank D fighters may outrank a faction of a hundred Rank F fighters
if its achievement history is deeper and its territorial footing is more
established. The System is not counting heads. It is evaluating presence,
performance, and demonstrated permanence.

For the leader, the faction is the amplifier. It extends reach, compresses
timelines, opens doors that personal power alone cannot open. It also
creates obligations, costs, and vectors of vulnerability that a lone
individual does not carry. Building a faction is not just acquiring resources.
It is choosing to be responsible for other people and everything that
responsibility invites in.

This is the personal cost that runs beneath the power. Jay can grow faster
alone. He cannot build anything that lasts alone. The faction is the
structure through which he becomes something that survives beyond any
single engagement. It is also the first thing in his life that can be
used against him in ways that raw strength cannot answer.

================================================================================
SECTION 1 — FACTION TYPES
================================================================================

The System recognizes five faction types. Type determines the faction's
structural identity, the category of achievements the System tracks most
closely, and the long-term ceiling of certain faction-specific benefits.
Type is declared at founding and cannot be changed after System registration.

Choosing a type is not merely administrative. It is a statement about what
the faction is for. The System reads that statement and calibrates its
recognition accordingly.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
COMBAT FACTION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Built around military strength and combat capability. The System tracks
kill records, over-rank engagements, territorial defense outcomes, and
engagement survival rates. Combat factions are the most common type in
newly integrated worlds in the immediate aftermath of Ascension, when
survival requires force and nothing else has had time to organize.

Primary benefits:
  Combat achievement multipliers for faction rank advancement. Access to
  System-issued combat contracts and bounties at lower rank thresholds
  than other faction types. Commander's Presence and Vanguard Aura skill
  potency increased by 15% while the faction holds Combat designation.

Ceiling consideration:
  Combat factions advance quickly through early ranks and plateau at mid-tier
  without diversifying their capability base. Exclusive combat identity
  becomes a vulnerability at higher faction ranks where political and
  economic leverage begins determining outcomes that force alone cannot.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
TRADE FACTION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Built around the acquisition, movement, and exchange of resources. The
System tracks trade volume, contract completion rates, resource node control,
and economic territory. Trade factions are rare in the immediate post-Ascension
period because there is little to trade and few structures to trade through.
They become progressively more powerful as the world stabilizes and resource
demand diversifies.

Primary benefits:
  Resource acquisition rates improved across controlled territory. System
  contracts for rare material acquisition appear at reduced threshold requirements.
  Faction members receive a passive bonus to Wisdom and Dexterity from
  faction affiliation that scales with faction rank.

Ceiling consideration:
  Trade factions lack independent defensive capability at early ranks and
  depend on allied combat factions or mercenary arrangements for territorial
  protection. The wealth they accumulate makes them targets. Managing that
  vulnerability is the defining strategic challenge of the type.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
RESEARCH FACTION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Built around knowledge acquisition, System analysis, and the development
of new techniques, formations, or understanding of post-Ascension phenomena.
The System tracks discovery achievements, knowledge compilation depth, and
the production of usable outputs — techniques, formations, equipment, or
intelligence that other factions act on. Research factions are the rarest
type at early faction ranks because the post-Ascension world does not yet
understand what it has access to, and most survivors are focused on
immediate survival rather than systematic study.

Primary benefits:
  Discovery EXP multiplied for all faction members. System-issued analysis
  contracts appear earlier. Members receive passive Wisdom and Mystery bonuses
  from faction affiliation. The faction unlocks a System-maintained knowledge
  archive that grows with contribution and persists beyond member turnover.

Ceiling consideration:
  Research factions are the slowest to generate immediate combat output and
  the slowest to achieve early faction rank advancement. Their power curve is
  the most back-weighted of any type. At high faction ranks, a well-developed
  research faction produces leverage that no other faction type can replicate,
  but the early arc requires tolerance for vulnerability and the discipline
  to invest in something that will not pay out immediately.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
GOVERNANCE FACTION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Built around territorial administration, population management, and the
maintenance of social order within a defined area. The System tracks the
size of the population under the faction's jurisdiction, the stability
metrics of that population, the resolution of internal disputes, and the
delivery of protection and resource access to registered members. Governance
factions do not fight for territory. They administer it once it has been
secured, and their power comes from the depth and stability of what they
control rather than their capacity to take more.

Primary benefits:
  Faction size caps raised significantly at each rank tier compared to
  other types. Member EXP bonuses for non-combat activities are higher.
  System-issued governance contracts offer substantial faction rank advancement
  credit. The faction unlocks a System-administered taxation mechanic that
  passively generates resources from registered territory.

Ceiling consideration:
  Governance factions without a military arm are dependent on allied
  combat factions for defense. The size advantages they enjoy also mean
  internal management complexity scales faster. Leadership skills from
  the Tier 2 and Tier 3 sets are not optional for this type — they are
  operationally necessary.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
HYBRID FACTION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
A faction that does not commit to a single primary identity. The System
recognizes hybrid status when a faction's achievement record shows
meaningful contribution across two or more type categories without
clear dominance in any one. Hybrid factions do not receive the specialized
bonuses of any single type but receive a reduced version of each relevant
type's benefits. They also receive a small passive bonus to faction rank
advancement rate that reflects the System's recognition of their breadth.

Primary benefits:
  Partial benefits from each active type category. Resistance to the ceiling
  vulnerabilities of single-type factions. Moderate bonus to faction rank
  advancement rate. Greater flexibility in which System contracts become
  accessible at each rank tier.

Ceiling consideration:
  Hybrid factions are harder to build well than any single-type faction.
  The breadth that protects them from single-type vulnerabilities also means
  they do not achieve the peak expression of any specialization. They require
  a leader capable of managing multiple domains simultaneously without losing
  coherence in any of them.

JAY'S FACTION TYPE: TBD — declared at founding. The arc direction suggests
Hybrid is the likely long-term shape, but the founding declaration should
reflect what Jay is building at the moment he registers, not what he intends
to build eventually. The System reads the present. The character's intentions
are his own.

================================================================================
SECTION 2 — FACTION FOUNDING
================================================================================

REQUIREMENTS FOR REGISTRATION
------------------------------
Founding a faction requires meeting a minimum threshold before the System
will accept the registration. The threshold is not high. The System is not
trying to prevent factions from forming. It is establishing that what is
being registered has enough substance to be worth tracking.

Minimum requirements at the time of registration:

  Members      : 3 individuals minimum, including the founding leader.
                 All must be System-integrated. All must consent to
                 registration — the System does not bind unwilling participants.

  Leader       : The registering individual must hold at least Rank F and
                 have completed their initial class assignment. Classless
                 individuals cannot register a faction.

  Name         : A faction name must be declared. Names are unique within
                 a universe. The System will reject any name already held
                 by a registered faction in Universe 711.

  Type         : A faction type must be declared. This cannot be changed
                 after registration.

  Territory    : Optional at founding. A faction can register without
                 claimed territory. Claiming territory after registration
                 is a separate process that contributes to faction rank
                 advancement but is not a founding prerequisite.

THE FOUNDING MOMENT
-------------------
When the registration conditions are met and submitted through the System
interface, the System processes the founding as a discrete event. It does
not happen passively. The founding leader actively initiates it.

What happens at the moment of registration:

  The System generates a faction entry in its record. This entry is visible
  to any integrated entity with a Faction Sense skill or equivalent. The
  faction now exists as a System entity distinct from its members.

  The founding leader receives the Founder achievement and, if the System
  judges the founding to be the first instance of its type or circumstance
  in Universe 711, additional achievement recognition may apply.

  The Tier 1 leadership skills become available to the founding leader
  immediately: Faction Call, Evaluation Eye, and Contract Binding unlock
  without any additional requirement.

  A faction rank of 0 is assigned. Rank 0 is not a functional rank. It is
  the state of having been registered but not yet having demonstrated enough
  to earn System recognition at even the lowest operational tier. The faction
  exists. It has not yet proven that it should be taken seriously.

WHAT FOUNDING COSTS JAY
------------------------
This belongs in the design layer, not the mechanical layer, but it is worth
naming explicitly.

Jay is not a person who builds things with other people. His entire formation
is individual. His father's philosophy was individual. The muscle memory of
his life is to rely on himself, assess others as variables, and carry the
weight of decisions alone.

A faction requires him to let other people's fates attach to his choices.
Every contract he signs, every mission he assigns, every enemy he makes by
existing as a faction leader rather than a lone actor, becomes a cost that
is paid partly by people who trusted him enough to register under his name.

The mechanical moment of founding is simple. The personal weight of what
it means is the story. These two things should not be collapsed into each
other. The System asks for three members and a name. What Jay is actually
agreeing to is something the System does not measure.

================================================================================
SECTION 3 — FACTION RANK STRUCTURE
================================================================================

OVERVIEW
--------
Faction rank is separate from the personal ranks of its members. It is
determined by a combination of five weighted factors the System evaluates
continuously. Rank advancement is not triggered by crossing a single
threshold. All five factors must meet their minimum for the target rank
before the System will recognize the advancement.

THE FIVE FACTORS
----------------

  MEMBERSHIP DEPTH
  The total number of registered members and the distribution of their
  personal ranks. Raw headcount matters less than the quality distribution.
  A faction of fifty Rank F members counts for less than a faction of
  ten Rank E members with three Rank D members at the top. The System
  weights the highest personal ranks most heavily and treats a steep
  pyramid as a structural indicator of ceiling.

  TERRITORIAL FOOTPRINT
  The amount of System-recognized territory the faction controls or
  administers. Territory can be claimed by clearing and holding it,
  by System contract completion in a given area, by agreement with
  other recognized factions, or by governance achievement within a
  defined zone. The System distinguishes between claimed territory and
  administered territory. Claimed means the faction has asserted presence.
  Administered means the faction has demonstrated sustained management.
  Administered territory counts for more.

  ACHIEVEMENT RECORD
  The cumulative weight of the faction's achievement history. This is
  not just the leader's personal achievements. It includes contributions
  from all members acting under the faction's banner. Over-rank kills
  as a faction. Contract completions above the faction's current rank
  expectation. Firsts. Survival events the System considers notable.
  The achievement record is the single most variable factor — it can
  advance dramatically in a single engagement or accrue slowly through
  consistent output over time.

  RESOURCE BASE
  What the faction controls in terms of material wealth, production
  capacity, and supply infrastructure. The System measures this in
  terms of faction-held resources, active income sources, and the
  faction's ability to sustain its current membership at their current
  operational tempo. A faction that is wealthy but cannot maintain
  its members scores lower than a faction that runs lean but never
  misses obligations.

  SYSTEM RECOGNITION
  The formal recognition tier the System has assigned based on the
  faction's interaction with System-issued structures. This includes
  the completion of System-issued faction quests, the resolution of
  System-arbitrated disputes, and the faction's standing within any
  System-recognized inter-faction framework in Universe 711. System
  Recognition cannot be manufactured or bought. It accrues through
  action and the System's own judgment of what that action means.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
RANK 0 — REGISTERED
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The state of having been founded and accepted into the System's record.
No minimum thresholds have been demonstrated yet.

What exists at this rank:
  A System entry. A name. A founding leader. Three or more members.
  Tier 1 leadership skills unlocked for the leader.
  Faction Call, Evaluation Eye, and Contract Binding are available.

What does not exist:
  Any formal System recognition. No protection under System-sanctioned
  inter-faction frameworks. No access to faction-tier System contracts.
  No territorial claims recognized by the System. The faction can be
  destroyed without System consequence to the aggressor.

The period between registration and Rank 1 is the most dangerous phase
of any faction's existence. It is publicly visible in the System's record
but carries no weight. Any established faction can move against it without
triggering any System-level response. The Rank 0 faction does not yet
matter enough to protect.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
RANK 1 — PROVISIONAL
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The first level of meaningful System recognition. The faction has
demonstrated enough to be treated as a real entity within the world's
power structure, not merely a registered name.

Minimum thresholds required:

  Membership Depth     : 5 registered members minimum. At least 2 must
                         be Rank E or higher.

  Territorial Footprint: At least one piece of territory claimed and
                         held for a minimum of 30 days without loss.
                         A single safe zone, resource node, or
                         administrative area qualifies.

  Achievement Record   : A minimum achievement weight that reflects
                         the faction having survived real adversity.
                         The System does not publish this number. The
                         intent is that the faction must have done
                         something under pressure, not just existed.

  Resource Base        : Sufficient to maintain current members at
                         basic operational capacity for 60 days
                         without external input.

  System Recognition   : Completion of one System-issued faction quest
                         at or above the faction's current strength
                         estimate.

What changes at Rank 1:
  The faction enters the System's inter-faction framework. Unprovoked
  aggression against a Rank 1 faction by a higher-ranked faction now
  carries System-registered consequences. Not prohibitive consequences
  at this stage, but the fact of the aggression is recorded and weighted
  in future System recognition for the aggressor.

  The leader gains access to Tier 2 loyalty skills once membership has
  passed the initial threshold and the first internal challenge has been
  navigated. Rally, Shared Will, and Oath of the Faction become available.

  The faction receives a System-assigned identifier visible in the
  public record. Other integrated entities can search for it, evaluate
  its public achievement record, and assess its territorial footprint.
  The faction is now a known quantity. Being a known quantity is useful.
  It is also a different kind of exposure than obscurity.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
RANK 2 — ESTABLISHED
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The faction has moved beyond provisional status. It has demonstrated
sustained function, not just initial survival. The System treats it as
a permanent fixture in the world's power landscape until proven otherwise.

Minimum thresholds required:

  Membership Depth     : 15 registered members minimum. At least one
                         must be Rank D or higher. Distribution must
                         show at least 5 Rank E members.

  Territorial Footprint: A minimum of three distinct areas held or
                         administered. At least one must be actively
                         administered rather than merely claimed.
                         Total footprint equivalent to a small district
                         or settlement zone.

  Achievement Record   : Achievement weight reflecting consistent
                         faction-level output over a sustained period.
                         Over-rank kills as a coordinated faction unit,
                         contract completions that exceeded the faction's
                         expected capability, or a single standout event
                         the System records as significant.

  Resource Base        : Self-sustaining for 120 days without external
                         input. Active income source in operation.

  System Recognition   : Completion of three System-issued faction quests
                         total. At least one must have been rated above
                         the faction's estimated capability at the time
                         of completion.

What changes at Rank 2:
  The faction gains access to System-issued contracts reserved for
  Established factions. These contracts are more lucrative and more
  dangerous than entry-level contracts. They also come with reputation
  weight. Completing them is visible to any faction with Threat Assessment
  or equivalent skills.

  Other factions begin forming opinions. The Rank 2 threshold is where
  inter-faction dynamics stop being theoretical and start being active.
  Recruitment overtures from rival factions to the leader's members may
  begin. Territorial pressure from factions that want what the faction
  has claimed is now a real consideration.

  The faction's name carries enough weight that unaffiliated individuals
  will have heard it. Not as a feared quantity, but as a real one.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
RANK 3 — RECOGNIZED
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The faction is a significant actor in its region. The System's recognition
at this tier reflects that the faction has become part of the world's
structure, not just an element within it. Established factions take it into
account when making decisions. Unaffiliated individuals actively seek
affiliation with it.

Minimum thresholds required:

  Membership Depth     : 50 registered members minimum. At least one
                         Rank C member required. Minimum 10 Rank D members.
                         The System begins evaluating combat capability
                         distribution as an independent metric at this rank.

  Territorial Footprint: Sufficient to constitute a recognizable presence
                         in the System's territorial map. Control of or
                         administration over what amounts to a small city
                         or equivalent resource zone. Multiple nodes, not
                         just contiguous territory.

  Achievement Record   : The faction must have resolved at least one
                         significant external conflict through either
                         military outcome or negotiated settlement
                         recognized by the System. Surviving a serious
                         external threat counts. Eliminating one counts
                         more.

  Resource Base        : Operationally independent. The faction can fund
                         expansion without drawing down its reserve. Active
                         surplus generation.

  System Recognition   : The faction must be acknowledged by at least two
                         other Rank 2 or higher factions through System-
                         registered agreements. Alliance, non-aggression, or
                         trade agreements all qualify. The System requires
                         that other factions have formally recognized this
                         one's existence in writing through the interface.

What changes at Rank 3:
  Tier 3 combat command skills become fully available: Commander's Presence,
  Battle Coordination, and Vanguard Aura. These unlock through combat
  leadership achievements and are not simply awarded on rank advancement,
  but Rank 3 is when the achievement thresholds that unlock them become
  reachable.

  The faction can now sponsor individual members for accelerated class
  advancement. The System recognizes faction sponsorship as a qualifying
  resource for challenge scenarios, reducing the threshold requirements
  for sponsored individuals.

  Inter-faction political visibility is now significant. The faction's
  decisions create ripple effects visible to other factions' intelligence
  infrastructure. This is the rank at which having enemies who actively
  plan against the faction rather than simply responding to it becomes
  the normal condition.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
RANK 4 — POWERFUL
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The faction is a regional power. Its decisions shape the environment around
it rather than simply responding to it. Other factions position relative
to it as a fixed point rather than as one variable among many.

Minimum thresholds required:

  Membership Depth     : 200 members minimum. Multiple Rank C members
                         required. Minimum one Rank B member OR the leader
                         must have reached Rank B. The System evaluates
                         peak personal rank as an independent factor
                         at this tier.

  Territorial Footprint: Control or administration of territory sufficient
                         to constitute a city-scale presence or equivalent
                         multi-zone resource network. The System expects
                         both breadth and depth at this tier. Having
                         many claimed territories with shallow administration
                         scores lower than fewer territories managed well.

  Achievement Record   : The faction's achievement record must include at
                         least one event the System classifies as having
                         changed the local power landscape. Destroying a
                         rival faction, resolving a large-scale System
                         event, or establishing a precedent that other
                         factions have had to respond to all qualify.

  Resource Base        : Sufficiently developed that resource management
                         is a strategic consideration, not a survival one.
                         The faction is no longer asking whether it can
                         afford to act. It is asking which actions it
                         should prioritize.

  System Recognition   : Acknowledged by factions and entities at or above
                         Rank 3. The faction's name must appear in multiple
                         System-registered agreements as a counterparty,
                         not just as a cosignatory. The System is evaluating
                         whether the faction has standing, not just presence.

What changes at Rank 4:
  Tier 4 negotiation and authority skills become available: Iron Terms,
  Threat Assessment, and Diplomatic Immunity unlock through the conditions
  tied to this rank's achievement requirements.

  The faction becomes eligible for System-issued world-event contracts.
  These are not regular contracts. They are the System's mechanism for
  directing significant actors toward significant problems. They are not
  optional in the way that standard contracts are optional. The System
  does not force participation, but declining them has its own costs.

  The leader's personal Title history begins intersecting with the
  faction's social weight. Legendary Titles earned by the leader create
  passive recognition effects that extend to the faction's reputation.
  The faction begins accumulating its own Titles independent of the leader.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
RANK 5 — DOMINANT  |  and beyond
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The faction has become a world-shaping force. Its existence is a structural
fact of Universe 711's post-Ascension political landscape. At this tier and
above, faction rank advancement is no longer primarily a matter of meeting
thresholds. It is a matter of the System recognizing that the faction has
become something that the world has no choice but to organize around.

Rank 5 and higher faction ranks are outside the scope of Book 1.
The thresholds, mechanics, and world-level implications of these tiers will
be developed as the story reaches them.

What can be established now: the arc from Rank 0 to Rank 4 is the core
of Jay's faction-building story through the early and middle arcs. Rank 4
represents a faction that can stand in the same room as the most powerful
organized groups in Universe 711 and be taken seriously. Reaching it is
the external objective. What Jay becomes in the process of reaching it is
the actual story.

================================================================================
SECTION 4 — FACTION ADVANCEMENT MECHANICS
================================================================================

HOW RANK ADVANCES
-----------------
Faction rank does not advance automatically when thresholds are crossed.
The System evaluates the faction's standing on a rolling basis and generates
an advancement notification when all five factors for the target rank are met
simultaneously. The leader can also manually request an evaluation through
the System interface. A manual evaluation that fails provides feedback on
which factors are insufficient but does not penalize the faction.

Rank advancement generates a System notification equivalent to an Epic-tier
achievement for the leader personally. The event is visible to other factions
with sufficient intelligence infrastructure. Advancing publicly draws attention.
There is no private advancement.

HOW RANK DECREASES
------------------
Faction rank can decrease. The System does not lock ranks in place once earned.
If the five factors fall sufficiently below the threshold for the current rank,
the System will issue a demotion warning and then a demotion if the deficiency
is not corrected within a set period.

What causes rank to drop: sustained member loss without replacement, territorial
loss without recovery, resource depletion below operational threshold, or
a failure so significant that the achievement record's accumulated weight no
longer offsets the faction's current state.

Demotion is rarer than advancement but it happens. A faction that loses its
highest-ranked member to death or defection, or that loses a major territorial
holding in a single catastrophic engagement, is at risk. The System is not
sentimental. It evaluates what is, not what was.

FACTION QUESTS
--------------
The System issues faction quests as a separate track from personal quests.
They are accessible only through the faction interface and visible only to
the registered leader and officers designated by the leader.

Faction quest tiers correspond loosely to faction rank, with contracts
available one tier above and one tier below the faction's current standing.
Accepting a contract above the faction's rank carries higher risk but
generates disproportionate achievement record weight on completion.

Faction quest types:

  CLEARANCE  : Eliminate a defined threat within a defined zone.
               Time-gated. All faction members who participated receive
               combat EXP and the faction receives achievement credit.

  ACQUISITION: Secure a defined resource or location within a time window.
               Often competitive with other registered factions. The System
               does not bar multiple factions from accepting the same
               acquisition contract. The first to complete claims the reward.

  DEFENSE    : Hold a defined area against escalating System-generated
               pressure for a defined duration. No external competition.
               Pure survival. The reward scales with how long the faction
               held and what quality of pressure it held against.

  GOVERNANCE : Administer a defined population or territory and meet
               stability metrics over a defined period. Low immediate
               danger. High faction rank advancement credit. The
               most underutilized contract type in the post-Ascension
               period because most factions at early ranks lack the
               membership depth to spare people for non-combat roles.

  DIPLOMATIC : Broker a System-recognized agreement between two defined
               parties. Success requires both parties to sign through
               the interface. The faction serves as intermediary and
               guarantor. The System holds the faction partially
               accountable if agreements it brokered are broken.

INTER-FACTION AGREEMENTS
-------------------------
Factions can enter into System-registered agreements with other registered
factions. Agreements are formal documents submitted through the interface.
The System records them, enforces their terms where mechanically possible,
and assigns breach penalties where terms are violated.

Agreement types:

  NON-AGGRESSION: Both factions commit to not initiating hostile action
                  against the other or the other's members for a defined
                  period. The System records any breach and applies
                  reputation penalties to the breaching faction's
                  standing with the System recognition factor.

  ALLIANCE      : A mutual commitment to assist the other party in defined
                  circumstances. Alliance terms vary. Full military alliance,
                  intelligence-sharing alliance, and resource alliance are
                  all distinct agreement structures. The obligations must
                  be specified. The System does not fill in ambiguous terms
                  charitably.

  TRADE         : Exchange terms for defined resources at defined intervals.
                  The System tracks fulfillment and generates breach records
                  on non-delivery. Trade agreements are the primary driver
                  of the resource base factor for factions without direct
                  resource node control.

  SUBORDINATION : A formal acknowledgment that one faction operates under
                  the authority of another. The subordinate faction retains
                  its registration and its own rank track but is recognized
                  by the System as operating within the superior faction's
                  political structure. Subordination is voluntary at the
                  moment of signing. It is not always voluntary in practice
                  by the time it is offered.

================================================================================
SECTION 5 — INTER-FACTION POLITICS AND WAR
================================================================================

THE SYSTEM'S ROLE IN CONFLICT
------------------------------
The System does not prevent factions from fighting each other. It does not
adjudicate most conflicts. What it does is record, categorize, and apply
consequence to actions that fall outside the framework it has established.

The System cares about one thing in inter-faction conflict: whether the
parties involved were operating within or outside the rules it recognizes.
It does not care who wins. It cares whether the conduct of the conflict
was legitimate in its terms.

Legitimate conflict, in the System's framework, is conflict between factions
that have not signed a non-aggression agreement, conducted in a way that
does not target non-integrated individuals, and not initiated through
deception or fraud that the System can verify. Within those parameters,
factions can destroy each other without any System consequence beyond the
standard record of what happened.

Outside those parameters, the System has tools. It does not deploy them
emotionally and it does not deploy them quickly. But a faction that builds
a pattern of conduct the System classifies as illegitimate will find that
pattern accumulating weight in its System recognition factor and eventually
encountering consequences that arrive without warning and without appeal.

TERRITORIAL CONFLICT
--------------------
Territory changes hands through three mechanisms:

  MILITARY SEIZURE  : A faction defeats the defending faction's presence
                      in a territory and establishes its own continuous
                      presence for a minimum period. The System does not
                      recognize a seized territory until the holding period
                      is completed. The period scales with the territory's
                      registered importance. A minor resource node requires
                      three days of uncontested presence. A major settlement
                      zone requires thirty days. Until the holding period
                      completes, the territory is in contested status and
                      the prior holder's claim is not fully extinguished.

  NEGOTIATED TRANSFER: A territory can change hands through a System-registered
                      agreement signed by both parties. The transfer is
                      immediate on execution. No holding period required.
                      Negotiated transfers are binding and cannot be
                      reversed unilaterally.

  ABANDONMENT       : A faction that ceases to maintain presence in a
                      territory for a defined period without explicitly
                      transferring it loses the System's recognition of
                      its claim. The territory reverts to unclaimed status
                      and is available for any faction to claim. Abandonment
                      timelines scale with territory importance.

FACTION WAR
-----------
A faction war is a System-registered state of open conflict between two
factions. It is distinct from ordinary territorial skirmishes in that it
is formally declared, recorded, and governed by a specific set of System
parameters that differ from standard inter-faction conflict rules.

Declaration of faction war requires a formal submission through the interface
by at least one party. The opposing faction receives a System notification.
The war state does not require mutual consent to begin. One party can
declare. The other party's lack of declaration does not mean the conflict
is not recognized as a war by the System.

What a declared faction war enables that ordinary conflict does not:
  The declaring faction can act against any registered member of the opposing
  faction without triggering standard social and reputation penalties from
  the System. Ordinarily, attacking another faction's members in territory
  not in active dispute carries some System-registered consequence. Under
  war declaration, this protection is suspended.

What a declared faction war costs:
  The war is public. Every other registered faction can see it in the
  System record. It signals vulnerability and distraction. Other factions
  that have been waiting for an opening may find the war state creates one.
  Neutral factions that had no interest in either party may develop one
  when the conflict's territorial and resource implications become clear.

War resolution:
  A declared faction war ends through one of three outcomes.

  Total dissolution of one faction. If a faction loses all registered members
  or its leader is killed and no succession structure exists within the
  interface, the System dissolves the faction. Its territory reverts to
  unclaimed. Its agreements are voided. It ceases to exist in the System's
  record.

  Surrender through the interface. The surrendering faction's leader can
  submit a formal capitulation. The terms of surrender must be negotiated
  and signed by both parties within a defined window or the System applies
  default terms that favor the declaring party.

  Negotiated ceasefire. Both parties submit and sign a ceasefire agreement.
  The war state is suspended. If the underlying terms are not resolved,
  the ceasefire can be broken and the war re-declared without the standard
  cooling period that applies to first declarations.

THE REAL COST OF WAR FOR A SMALL FACTION
-----------------------------------------
This belongs in the design layer alongside the founding section, for the
same reason.

A Rank 1 faction that declares war on a Rank 3 faction is announcing that
it is willing to be destroyed. The System's framework does not protect small
factions from existential threats that emerge from their own decisions.
Jay's faction, in the early arc, does not have the standing to initiate
conflict against major powers without accepting that the cost may be the
people who trusted him.

This tension is one of the faction system's central pressures on Jay
as a character. He is used to absorbing costs himself. The faction means
those costs are no longer only his to absorb.

================================================================================
SECTION 6 — FACTION STRUCTURE AND ROLES
================================================================================

OVERVIEW
--------
The System recognizes a basic internal hierarchy for registered factions.
The specific names of roles within that hierarchy are the leader's choice.
The functional designations are fixed. The System operates on the functional
layer regardless of what the leader calls the positions.

FIXED FUNCTIONAL ROLES

  LEADER
  The individual who registered the faction or who holds the succession
  designation. The only individual with full access to all faction interface
  functions, including dissolution, war declaration, subordination agreements,
  and succession transfer. There is exactly one Leader at any time. If the
  Leader dies without having designated a successor through the interface,
  the faction enters a dissolution countdown. The countdown period scales
  with faction rank. A Rank 1 faction has 7 days. A Rank 4 faction has 30.

  OFFICER
  Individuals designated by the Leader with partial interface access.
  Officers can view faction contracts, access the faction's internal
  System records, and exercise some Contract Binding functions on behalf
  of the faction within the scope the Leader has defined. The number of
  Officer slots available scales with faction rank: 2 at Rank 1, 5 at Rank 2,
  10 at Rank 3, 20 at Rank 4.

  MEMBER
  Any registered individual who has signed a Contract Binding or equivalent
  System-recognized agreement with the faction. Members receive the passive
  benefits of faction affiliation at their rank tier and contribute to all
  five advancement factors through their actions while operating under the
  faction banner. Members can leave through a formal interface process.
  Leaving mid-contract triggers whatever penalty terms the Contract Binding
  specified.

  PROVISIONAL MEMBER
  A member in a defined evaluation period. The System allows a leader to
  register individuals as Provisional for up to 60 days before requiring
  a full Contract Binding or releasing them. Provisional members receive
  reduced faction affiliation benefits and contribute at a lower rate to
  the advancement factors. They cannot access Officer-level interface
  functions regardless of the Leader's designation.

SUCCESSION
----------
The Leader can designate a successor through the interface at any time.
The successor must be a registered Officer. The designation can be changed
at any time before it is triggered. If the Leader dies or is incapacitated
and has a valid succession designation, the designated individual assumes
the Leader role immediately. All interface functions transfer with the role.

The new Leader's personal stats and skills are irrelevant to the transfer.
The System does not evaluate whether the successor is qualified. It executes
the transfer the Leader authorized. The political reality of whether the
faction will follow the successor is not a System problem.

================================================================================
DESIGN NOTES
================================================================================

  The faction system is intentionally designed to create pressure at every
  rank. Each tier introduces new vulnerabilities at the same time as it
  provides new capabilities. This mirrors the story's core tension: power
  and cost scaling together rather than power neutralizing cost.

  Jay's faction is a Rank 0 entity at the start of Arc 1. Every element of
  the system above is something he will encounter through the story rather
  than something he knows going in. His understanding of what he has built
  will develop in parallel with the faction itself. This is intentional.
  He should not start with a mastery of faction politics. He should learn
  it the hard way, which is the only way his character actually learns anything.

  The five advancement factors are designed to resist pure optimization.
  A leader who maximizes membership while ignoring territorial footprint
  will hit a wall. A leader who focuses entirely on achievement record
  without building resource base sustainability will find the faction
  fragile in exactly the moments it needs to be durable. Jay's strengths
  map strongly onto achievement record and territorial footprint through
  combat. His weaknesses map onto governance and resource base. Managing
  that gap requires either developing personal capabilities he does not
  currently have or finding members who cover what he cannot.

  The latter is the more likely path. And building genuine trust in people
  who cover his gaps is one of the most specific and uncomfortable things
  the faction system will ask of him.

  Samantha's role within the faction is TBD but the design space for her
  is in the governance and resource domain, counterbalancing exactly
  the areas Jay is structurally weakest in.
